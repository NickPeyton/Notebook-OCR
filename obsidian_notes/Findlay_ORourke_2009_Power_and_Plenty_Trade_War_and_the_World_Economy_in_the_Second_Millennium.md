## Chapter 6: Trade and the Industrial Revolution

*   **Page 313:** Late 18th and early 19th century: 0.5% Gross Domestic Product growth, 7% growth in cotton, approximately 3% iron/coal. Smallish rates, but big structural changes (61-29% of workers in agriculture). 1700-1841. Huge rise in population without drop in living standards equals escape from Malthus, enter Solow growth.
*   **Page 321:** Transport technology and colonies based land constraints by shipping settlers to Neo-Europes.
*   **Page 324:** Huge deindustrialization in India (1750-1900). Started in 0.4% ended in 0.2% of United Kingdom.
*   **Page 330:** Food imports allowed United Kingdom population to rise while United Kingdom specialized in industrial goods. Increased population also increased military power.
*   **Page 333:** Supply of United Kingdom manufacturing goods rose way faster than demand, substantially lowering worldwide prices.
*   **Page 338:** United Kingdom and regulatory differed from previous Smithian growth spurts because it was sustained and technological.
*   **Page 342:** New World provided elastic land supply, Africa provided elastic labor - both key to Industrial Revolution.
*   Exports allow huge production increase w/o huge domestic price drop - good for young industry.
*   **Page 349:** No link between interest rates and Glorious Revolution - no investor security.
*   **Page 356:** "Oriental Despotism" extracted way, less tax as percentage of Gross Domestic Product than leading Western European states.
*   **Page 357:** Calicut and Malacca more laissez-faire than European states.
*   399. Political fragmentation also present in India, Southeast Asia: encouraged military innovation but made them exploitable.
*   300. Coastline, good winds/currents, proximity to America = geographical advantage of Western Europe.
*   Summary: Trade-colonization combination = critical to Industrial Revolution. Huge markets allow huge production increases to be worthwhile, and markets are opened and protected through wars.
*   Offloading surplus population, colonizing new land, buying elastically-supplied slaves allowed Industrial Revolution to continue + create take-off.
