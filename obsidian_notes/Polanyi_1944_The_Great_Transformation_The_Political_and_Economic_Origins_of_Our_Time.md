# The Great Transformation: The Political and Economic Origins of Our Time

## I - The Hundred Years' Peace
*   Haute Finance = first truly powerful "peace lobby," able to prevent Great-Power conflicts (save with small colonial wars) by linking international politics very directly to national economies. Failed as Europe solidified into two enemy blocs around 1900.

## II - Conservative Twenties, Revolutionary Thirties
*   Huge efforts to restore gold standard and resume normal liberal political economy => 1920s caused restriction of trade and capital flows = temporary "autarky."
*   Example in low US dollar rates raised to maintain value of Great British Pound, because lower US dollar inflation. Germany and Union of Soviet Socialist Republics well placed to profit from its collapse and final death of 19th-century institutions.

## III - Habitation Versus Improvement
*   "Futile" Tudor and Stuart anti-enclosure measures slowed change and thereby made it bearable. Productive, complex machines require and create market system to secure inputs and market for outputs.

## IV - Societies and Economic Systems
*   No real pre-industrial (pre-modern?) "market economies"; social esteem = overriding goal of individuals (even self-interested ones). Reciprocity, redistribution, householding = main forms of economic life.

## V - Evolution of the Market Pattern
*   Pre-industrial local (town+country) and long-distance trade essentially uncompetitive, only "internal" / "national" trade had corrosive market effects. Internal trade created by governments to marshal society's resources in Early Modern period, but still constrained by governments.

## VI - The Self-Regulating Market and the Fictitious Commodities
*   Land, Labor, Money, not commodities produced for sale like others. Previously regulated by social and legal structures. Now treated as "commodities" because of the demand of complex machines.

# VII. Speenhamland, 1795-1834, United Kingdom
*   Laborers "protected" by Speenhamland system, wage subsidies paid from poor rates. The system created very low wages, indigence, but "protected" poor from the labor market.

# VIII. Antecedents and Consequences
*   The turn of the 19th century saw a rise in unemployment and visible poverty because of fluctuations of foreign trade. Now, hit harder. Speenhamland was destructive, but its repeal was a sudden, violent, and traumatic shock.

# IX. Pauperism, Utopia
*   Poverty versus very difficult: subsidize and encourage sloth, or employ and compete with private business?

# X. Political Economy and the Discovery of Society
*   Different perspectives on poverty. Townsend = "goats and dogs" natural law.
*   Smith = gradual enrichment of workers.

## §2.2: Self-Protection of Society
*   Market liberalism often required aggressive intervention to maintain and was imposed most completely on those unable to defend themselves politically, that is, colonies.
*   This requires destroying traditional institutions of land and labor. In Europe, the vanguard was about forced parties across the spectrum to adopt laws blunting the force of land and labor markets. Tariffs = price of maintaining the gold standard and hideous unemployment at home.

## §3: Transformation In Progress
*   The old standard forced cuts to social services or to wages during the Great Depression. Only the United States and United Kingdom could afford to go off it and socially spend. Fascism was a response to conditions and arose everywhere.
*   Conservatives accomplished counter-revolutions domestically; fascists were brought to power by "national foreign policy." The way forward for democracies is the end of the market society, more central planning while maintaining and expanding the scope of guaranteed rights.
